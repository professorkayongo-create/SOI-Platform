Atomic Structure — Introduction

An atom is the smallest particle of an element that retains the chemical properties of that element.

Subatomic Particles

Protons have a positive charge and are found in the nucleus. Neutrons have no charge and are also found in the nucleus. Electrons have a negative charge and move around the nucleus in energy levels.

Atomic Number

The atomic number is the number of protons in the nucleus of an atom.

Periodic Table — Introduction

The periodic table organizes elements according to their atomic numbers and chemical properties.

Groups

Elements in the same group generally have similar chemical properties because they have similar numbers of outer-shell electrons.

Periods

A period is a horizontal row in the periodic table. The period number indicates the number of occupied electron shells.

Chemical Reactions — Introduction

A chemical reaction occurs when one or more substances change to form new substances.

Examples

Combustion, neutralization, displacement, decomposition, and combination are examples of chemical reactions.

Chemical Equations

Chemical equations represent chemical reactions using chemical symbols and formulas.

Acids and Bases — Introduction

Acids are substances that produce hydrogen ions in aqueous solution, while bases are substances that react with acids and may produce hydroxide ions in aqueous solution.

pH Scale

The pH scale is used to measure how acidic or alkaline a solution is. The scale commonly ranges from 0 to 14.

Neutralization

A neutralization reaction occurs when an acid reacts with a base to form salt and water.

Organic Chemistry — Introduction

Organic chemistry is the study of carbon compounds.

Hydrocarbons

Hydrocarbons are compounds made up of carbon and hydrogen atoms.

Examples

Important groups include alkanes, alkenes, alkynes, alcohols, and carboxylic acids.

Practical Chemistry — Introduction

Practical Chemistry involves carrying out experiments to investigate chemical substances and reactions.

Laboratory Safety

Always wear appropriate protective equipment, follow instructions carefully, handle chemicals responsibly, and keep the laboratory clean.
