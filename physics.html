Mechanics — Introduction

Mechanics is the branch of Physics concerned with the motion of objects and the forces acting on them.

Speed

Speed = Distance ÷ Time

Acceleration

Acceleration = Change in Velocity ÷ Time

Newton's Second Law

F = ma

Where F is force, m is mass, and a is acceleration.

Heat & Temperature — Introduction

Heat is energy transferred from a hotter object to a colder object because of a temperature difference.

Methods of Heat Transfer

The three main methods are conduction, convection, and radiation.

Specific Heat Capacity

Q = mcΔT

Where Q is heat energy, m is mass, c is specific heat capacity, and ΔT is the change in temperature.

Waves — Introduction

A wave is a disturbance that transfers energy from one place to another.

Wave Equation

v = fλ

Where v is wave speed, f is frequency, and λ is wavelength.

Examples of Waves

Examples include sound waves, water waves, light waves, radio waves, and other electromagnetic waves.

Electricity — Introduction

Electricity involves the movement and interaction of electric charges.

Ohm's Law

V = IR

Where V is voltage, I is current, and R is resistance.

Electrical Power

P = VI

Magnetism — Introduction

Magnetism is a force associated with magnetic fields produced by magnets and moving electric charges.

Electromagnets

An electromagnet is a temporary magnet produced when electric current flows through a coil of wire.

Applications

Electromagnets are used in electric motors, generators, loudspeakers, relays, and lifting machines.

Light & Optics — Introduction

Optics is the study of light and its behavior.

Reflection

Reflection occurs when light strikes a surface and changes direction.

Refraction

Refraction is the change in direction of light when it passes from one medium to another.

Lenses

Convex and concave lenses are used in optical devices such as cameras, microscopes, and spectacles.
