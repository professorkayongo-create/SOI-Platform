Number & Operations — Introduction

Numbers are used to represent quantities and perform calculations. Common types include natural numbers, whole numbers, integers, rational numbers, and real numbers.

Percentage

Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100

Ratio

A ratio compares two or more quantities of the same kind.

Algebra — Introduction

Algebra uses letters and symbols to represent unknown or changing quantities.

Example

If:

x + 5 = 12

Then:

x = 12 − 5 = 7

Factorization

Factorization involves expressing an algebraic expression as a product of simpler expressions.

Geometry — Introduction

Geometry is the study of shapes, sizes, positions, and properties of figures.

Important Facts

The angles in a triangle add up to:

180°

The angles around a point add up to:

360°

Mensuration — Introduction

Mensuration involves calculating measurements of geometric shapes.

Rectangle Area

Area = Length × Width

Triangle Area

Area = ½ × Base × Height

Circle Area

Area = πr²

Statistics — Introduction

Statistics involves collecting, organizing, analyzing, and interpreting data.

Mean

Mean = Sum of Values ÷ Number of Values

Median

The median is the middle value when data is arranged in order.

Mode

The mode is the value that occurs most frequently.

Trigonometry — Introduction

Trigonometry studies relationships between angles and sides of triangles.

SOH CAH TOA

sin θ = Opposite ÷ Hypotenuse

cos θ = Adjacent ÷ Hypotenuse

tan θ = Opposite ÷ Adjacent

These relationships are commonly used when solving right-angled triangle problems.
